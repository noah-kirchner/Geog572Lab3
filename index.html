Castelao Map

Noah Kirchner

This explainer describes a web map based on the minimalist, geometric abstraction style of Leandro Castelao

Inspired by Castelao's sleek, contemporary minimalist style, this map emphasizes human geography with bold, crisp lines on a palette that balances warmth, contrast, and modern freshness.

At small scales, the simple design draws from Castelao's solid orange and blue scheme, with crisp dark brown lines representing major roads.

Urban areas at larger scales, such as the island of Manhattan pictured here, emphasize Castelao's mixture of clean gray and orange lines. Urban grids accentuate Castelao's geometric design.

Rural areas, such as Vilas County, Wisconsin, make use of Castelao's lighter orange shades and translucent feel while maintaining his blue/orange contrast with water features.

Region-level scales make use of Castelao's moderate orange tones as state borders, large rivers, interstates, and state highways combine to mimic Castelao's use of solid intersecting lines.
